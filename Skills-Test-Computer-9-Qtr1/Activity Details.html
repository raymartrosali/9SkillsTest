Caffe Americano (also known as Americano or American) is a type of coffee drink prepared by diluting an espresso with hot water, giving it a similar strength to, but different flavor from, traditionally brewed coffee. The strength of an Americano varies with the number of shots of espresso and the amount of water added. The name is also spelled with varying capitalization and use of diacritics: e.g., cafe americano. In Italy, caffe americano could mean either espresso with hot water or filtered coffee (caffe all'americana). Ingredients: Hot water and espresso A caffe mocha , also called mocaccino , is a chocolate-flavored variant of a caffe latte. Other commonly used spellings are mochaccino and also mochachino. The name is derived from the city of Mocha, Yemen, which was one of the centers of early coffee trade. Ingredients: Chocolate, espresso, and hot milk
